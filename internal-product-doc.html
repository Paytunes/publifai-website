Kam+AI

Product Design Document

"The operating system for India's small businesses, delivered through a chat."

India has ~63 million small businesses. The chai stall owner, the neighborhood tutor, the freelance photographer, the Ayurvedic clinic, the local electrician. They all share the same problems: they need customers, they need to look professional, they need to get paid, they need to manage their time — and they have zero appetite for learning software.

What they do have is a phone with WhatsApp or Telegram open 14 hours a day. That's their CRM, their marketing channel, their invoicing system, their calendar. All duct-taped together with screenshots and voice notes.

Kam+AI is the thesis that every small business operation that currently requires a separate app, a separate login, a separate learning curve — can instead be a conversation.

The Problem Map

Getting Found Online

Most have no website. Maybe a Google Maps listing a cousin set up two years ago with the wrong phone number. The "solution" today is a ₹30K agency or a WordPress site they'll never update.

Looking Professional

No logo, no consistent branding, no digital business card. They share a blurry photo on WhatsApp as their "catalog." First impressions are lost before they begin.

Getting Paid

Invoicing is a WhatsApp message saying "bhaiya ₹2500 bhej do." No receipts, no tracking, no automated reminders. GST compliance is a nightmare handled by a CA they visit once a quarter.

Managing Time

A notebook or their memory. Double bookings happen. No-shows have no consequence because there's no system. Doctors, tutors, and salons lose hours every week.

Marketing

They know they should "post on Instagram" but have no idea what to post, when, or how. They pay ₹500 to some local guy who runs the same generic template for every shop.

Customer Relationships

They remember regulars by face. No record of who bought what, who hasn't visited in 3 months, who referred a friend. Every customer insight lives in their head.

The Thesis

The Insight

Small business owners don't want to "build websites" or "learn CRMs". They want customers. A conversational agent that handles everything — from setup to updates to payments — in the messaging app they already live in.

Agent Architecture

Each problem is an agent. Each agent is conversational, contextual, and backed by AI + human expertise. "Mera website update karo" → Website Agent. "Kal ka invoice bhejo" → Billing Agent. Same chat, one relationship that compounds over time.

Why Chat-First Wins in India

800M+ smartphones but low software literacy. Everyone can send a message. Native Hinglish/Hindi support. Trust in people over software. No app install — just share a Telegram link.

The Moat

Not any single agent. The moat is the relationship (3+ months of business context), the orchestration layer (6 agents sharing context), and the human network (curated designers, writers, CAs).

Business Model

₹9,999 one-time to build a professional website via chat. ₹999/mo for ongoing maintenance and updates. ~₹22K/year — cheaper than any agency, and the site stays alive. As trust deepens, add-on agents (booking, reviews, social) at ₹499-999/mo each. AI handles 90%+ of interactions — humans only step in for custom design. Like Shopify for Indian SMBs, but you never leave WhatsApp.

HIL QUEUE ARCHITECTURE

The orchestrator tracks which tasks were escalated and feeds this back to improve the AI over time.

Operational Playbook

Step-by-step execution guide: scrape leads, identify doctors without websites, generate personalized outreach, close deals, deliver a live website via WhatsApp.

1. Build Lead List (SCOUT)

Source 1: Practo (Primary)

India's largest healthcare directory. Structured data on every dermatologist: name, clinic, fees, reviews, experience, qualifications, services, phone.

Apify: apify.com/shahidirfan/practo-doctors-scraper — Config: speciality: "dermatologist", city: "[city]", results_wanted: 500. Run per city.

Fallback: Custom Selenium + BeautifulSoup script. Practo uses infinite scroll. Crawl profile pages for services, reviews, website URLs.

Source 2: Google Maps / GBP

Provides what Practo doesn't: actual website URL, Google rating, review count, phone, business hours, photos.

Outscraper (recommended) or Omkarcloud (free, self-hosted via Docker). Run 3-5 query variations per city: "dermatologist in [city]", "cosmetic dermatologist in [city]", "skin clinic in [city]", "aesthetic clinic in [city]".

Source 3: JustDial (Supplementary)

Has phone numbers and catches smaller clinics. More challenging to scrape (CAPTCHAs). Use manually (10-15 min per city) or buy JustDial lead packs directly.

Merge & Deduplicate

Load all CSVs into Pandas. Normalize names. Fuzzy match on doctor_name + city (fuzzywuzzy, threshold 85%). Prefer Google Maps for website_url and phone; Practo for qualifications and services. Output: master_dermatologists.csv with 2,000-5,000 unique records.
