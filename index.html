The Problem

India's small businesses are invisible online.

The chai stall owner, the neighbourhood tutor, the freelance photographer, the local clinic. They all need customers — and they have zero appetite for learning software.

🔍

Can't be found online

No website. Maybe a Google listing a cousin set up 2 years ago with the wrong phone number. New customers can't discover them.

💸

Solutions are too expensive

A freelancer charges ₹15K-40K for an English-only site. Agencies want ₹30K+. And maintenance is another ₹5K-20K/month.

🌐

Language barrier

Only 2-4% of Indians have professional English fluency. The web is 49.5% English, 0.07% Hindi. SMBs can't create content for it.

📱

Tools are too complex

Wix, GoDaddy, WordPress — all require dashboards, forms, and technical literacy. 94% of MSMEs are single-owner operations.

🏪

Platforms eat their profits

Zomato takes 25-35% commission. JustDial charges ₹18K-59K/year for mostly fake leads. They don't own their customer data.

📉

Social media stopped working free

Facebook organic reach: 1.37%. Instagram engagement down 18% YoY. The free lunch is over. SMBs need owned presence.

Why This Works in India

Conversational-first is a structural advantage.

💬 Comfort & Literacy

800M+ smartphone users, but software literacy is low outside metros. Everyone knows how to send a message. Not everyone knows how to navigate a dashboard.

🗣️ Language

Publifai handles Hinglish, Hindi, and regional languages natively. No UI to translate. The conversation IS the interface.

🤝 Trust

Indian SMBs trust people, not software. A conversational agent that remembers context and talks like a helpful assistant builds trust faster than any SaaS UI.

📲 Distribution

No app install. No signup flow. Share a WhatsApp link — that's the entire acquisition funnel. 97% of Indian SMBs are already there.
